Common Dental Emergencies - What to Do

🦷 Severe Toothache URGENT

Persistent, throbbing pain that doesn't respond to over-the-counter medication.

  • Rinse with warm salt water
  • Apply cold compress externally
  • Take recommended pain reliever
  • Call us immediately

🩸 Knocked-Out Tooth CRITICAL

Time is critical! We have the best chance of saving your tooth within 1 hour.

  • Handle tooth by crown only
  • Rinse gently if dirty
  • Try to reinsert or store in milk
  • Get to us within 30 minutes

🔨 Broken/Chipped Tooth URGENT

Sharp edges can cut your mouth and expose nerves causing severe pain.

  • Save any broken pieces
  • Rinse mouth with warm water
  • Cover sharp edges with wax
  • Apply gauze if bleeding

🤕 Dental Abscess CRITICAL

Infection that can spread to other parts of your body if untreated.

  • Rinse with salt water
  • Never attempt to drain
  • Take temperature
  • Seek immediate care
