Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need a root canal? +

Common signs include severe toothache, prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold, tooth discoloration, swelling and tenderness in nearby gums, and a persistent pimple on the gums. However, sometimes there are no symptoms, which is why regular checkups are important.

Is a root canal really painless? +

Yes! With modern anesthetics and techniques, root canal treatment is typically no more uncomfortable than getting a filling. Most patients report feeling immediate relief from the pain that brought them in. We also offer sedation options for anxious patients.

How long does a root canal take? +

Most root canals can be completed in 60-90 minutes. Complex cases may require a second visit. With our advanced technology, we've significantly reduced treatment time while improving outcomes.

What's the success rate of root canal treatment? +

Our root canal treatments have a 98% success rate. With proper care and restoration (usually a crown), a root canal-treated tooth can last a lifetime. We use advanced microscopes and 3D imaging to ensure thorough treatment.

Will I need a crown after my root canal? +

In most cases, yes. A crown protects the treated tooth from fracture and restores its full function. We'll discuss the best restoration option for your specific situation.

Is root canal treatment covered by insurance? +

Most dental insurance plans cover root canal treatment. Our team will help verify your benefits and provide a detailed cost estimate. We also offer flexible payment plans to make treatment affordable.

What happens if I don't get a root canal? +

Without treatment, the infection can spread to surrounding teeth and bone, potentially causing an abscess. This can lead to more serious health issues and eventual tooth loss. Early treatment is always easier and more successful.
